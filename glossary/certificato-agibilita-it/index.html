Certificato di Agibilità

The certificato di agibilità (or certificato di abitabilità) is Italy's certificate confirming a building meets hygiene, safety, and habitability standards required before it can be legally occupied.

What Is the Certificato di Agibilità?

The certificato di agibilità (habitability certificate, sometimes still called certificato di abitabilità for residential properties) is a document issued by the local municipality (comune) confirming that a building — or specific unit within a building — meets all applicable standards for human habitation. These standards cover structural safety, fire resistance, sanitary installations, natural lighting and ventilation, energy performance, and compliance with the building permit as approved. Without it, a property technically cannot be legally occupied.

Legal Requirement for Sales

Italian law requires that a habitability certificate (or documented evidence that one exists or has been applied for) must be referenced in the notarial deed of sale. Selling a property without one is not technically prohibited, but the buyer is entitled to terminate the contract or seek a price reduction if the certificate was not disclosed. In practice, notaries flag the absence of a certificate and buyers should consider it a significant due-diligence risk requiring resolution.

When Is It Needed?

A new certificate is required upon completion of new construction, following substantial renovation works (works requiring a building permit), and when a property undergoes a significant change of use (e.g. commercial to residential). For older buildings constructed before 1 September 1967, the certificate was not required under the law then in force — such properties are often sold with a notarial declaration that no certificate was required at the time of construction.

Application Process

The developer or owner applies to the comune's urban planning office (ufficio urbanistica), submitting: the application form; the final technical report from the project's direttore dei lavori (site director); compliance certifications from installers (electrical, plumbing, heating — the dichiarazione di conformità under DM 37/2008); and the energy performance certificate (APE). The comune issues the certificate after checking documentation. Since 2020, a SCIA (simplified building works notification) scheme allows self-certification in some cases.

Agibilità and Mortgages

Most Italian banks require confirmation that a property has a valid agibilità certificate (or that the pre-1967 exemption applies) before disbursing a mortgage. A property without a certificate may still be financeable with a specific mortgage condition requiring the certificate within a set period post-disbursement. This is risky for buyers — it is preferable to make the certificate's existence a condition precedent in the compromesso.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying an older building assuming the pre-1967 exemption applies without verifying the actual construction date in the building permit files. Some buildings thought to be pre-1967 have later additions that require their own certificate.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Make the existence of the agibilità (or a clear legal explanation of its absence) a condition of the compromesso — don't leave it to be resolved at the rogito when your leverage is minimal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does agibilità mean the building is structurally safe?

It certifies regulatory compliance at the time of issue — it does not replace a structural survey or guarantee the current condition.

What if a property has never had agibilità?

It may still be sold, but the buyer has grounds to seek a price reduction or termination. A specialist geometra can assess the cost of regularisation.

Can I apply for agibilità myself as a buyer?

If the original developer defaulted or the property was built without one, the current owner can apply — engage a geometra or architect to manage the process.
