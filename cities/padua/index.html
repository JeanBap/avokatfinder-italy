English-Speaking Lawyers in Padua: Market Overview

Padua (Padova) — home to one of the world's oldest universities (founded 1222) and Giotto's sublime Scrovegni Chapel — is a prosperous Veneto city just 30 minutes from Venice by train. It offers significantly lower property prices than Venice while providing excellent infrastructure, a large international student community, and strong buy-to-let potential. Padua is increasingly popular with expats seeking a genuine Italian city with easy Venice access at a fraction of the price.

Expert Tip — Padua rental strategy tip:

Padua's rental market benefits from two distinct demand pools: long-term student lets (low yield, low management) and short-term tourist lets for Venice visitors (higher yield, more management). If you plan short-term lets, check the Comune di Padova's meublé de tourisme registration requirements and the regional CIR code (Veneto regional law) before purchasing. Zones near the historic centre may face future short-let restrictions as Venice's overtourism policies spread.

FAQs — Lawyers in Padua

What taxes apply to buying property in Padua?

Resale (second home): 9 % imposta di registro + €50 + €50. Prima casa: 2 %. New-build: IVA 10 % or 4 % prima casa. The notaio collects all at the rogito.

Is Padua a better investment than Venice?

For rental yield, yes. Venice residential property yields are suppressed by very high entry prices (€8,000–15,000/m²) and increasing short-let restrictions. Padua at €2,200–3,500/m² delivers gross yields of 4–6 % for student lets and 5–8 % for tourist lets, with lower entry cost and simpler management logistics. The Venice day-trip market sustains Padua short-let demand year-round.

How do I get an Italian codice fiscale in Padua?

A codice fiscale can be obtained at the Agenzia delle Entrate office in Padua (Via Egidio Forcellini area), any Italian consulate abroad, or via an avvocato with power of attorney. It is free and essential before signing any property contract, opening an Italian bank account, or applying for a permesso di soggiorno.

What short-let registration is needed for an Airbnb in Padua?

Short-term lets in Padua (Veneto) require: (1) registration with the Comune di Padova as a struttura ricettiva; (2) a Codice Identificativo Regionale (CIR) under Veneto regional law (LR 11/2013 and subsequent reforms); (3) payment of tassa di soggiorno collected from guests. Non-compliance risks fines. A local commercialista or avvocato sets up the registration.

Can a non-EU national buy property in Padua?

Yes — Italy's reciprocity principle applies. Most Western nationals may purchase freely. A codice fiscale is needed before signing contracts. For residency, non-EU buyers need an appropriate visa (Elective Residency, Investor, or employment-based). An immigration avvocato in Padua advises on the correct pathway.
